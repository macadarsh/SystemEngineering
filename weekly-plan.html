12-Week Study Plan

A week-by-week roadmap through the modules, with a small task each week to turn reading into practice. Adjust the pace to suit you.

How to use this plan

Each week pairs one or two modules with a short, concrete task. The goal is steady progress and a habit of applying ideas, not racing to the end. Use the review weeks to consolidate before moving on.

The plan

WeekFocusModule(s)Do this week
1OrientationIntroduction to Systems EngineeringRead Module 1; write your own one-line definition of a system.
2MindsetSystems Thinking & Core ConceptsSketch the boundary & interfaces of a system you know.
3The journeyThe SE Lifecycle & Process ModelsMap the Vee model onto a past project.
4NeedsStakeholder Needs & RequirementsWrite 5 requirements that pass the ‘good requirement’ test.
5StructureSystem Architecture & DesignDraw a functional vs physical view of one product.
6ModellingModelling & MBSE (SysML)Explore the four SysML pillars with a small example.
7ReviewModules 1–6Revision + first practice question set.
8Proving itIntegration, Verification & ValidationPick a requirement; choose a verification method.
9UncertaintyRisk & Decision ManagementBuild a small risk register with handling strategies.
10ControlConfiguration & Change ManagementDefine a baseline and a change-control flow.
11Leading the workTechnical & Project Management + Specialty EngineeringOutline a one-page SEMP; list the key ‘-ilities’.
12End of life & wrap-upOperations, Maintenance & DisposalFull revision + second practice set + a case study.

Tips for sticking with it

  • Protect a fixed slot each week — consistency beats intensity.
  • Always finish a week by doing, not just reading.
  • Keep a running list of questions to revisit in review weeks.
