INCOSE Exams & Certification

INCOSE’s SEP certification program and the knowledge exam that underpins it.

The SEP certification program

INCOSE certifies Systems Engineering Professionals (SEP) at three levels of increasing experience and demonstrated capability: ASEP, CSEP, and ESEP.

The three levels at a glance

LevelWho it is forKnowledge exam?Experience
ASEP
Associate SEP		Knowledgeable, typically junior systems engineers.Yes — pass the knowledge exam.No SE experience required.
CSEP
Certified SEP		Practising systems engineers.Yes — same exam as ASEP.~5 years of SE experience (with a qualifying degree) plus an application reviewed against references.
ESEP
Expert SEP		Senior systems engineers and leaders.No exam — by application.~25 years of recognised SE experience (20 if already a CSEP) plus demonstrated leadership.

The knowledge exam

ASEP and CSEP both require passing the same multiple-choice knowledge exam, drawn entirely from the INCOSE Systems Engineering Handbook. Since 15 March 2025 the exam is based wholly on the SEH Fifth Edition (2023). Every question is multiple choice with a single correct answer.

Format & logistics

  • Available by computer (online, remotely proctored) or on paper.
  • Standard computer exam: 120 questions in 120 minutes (100 scored, plus up to 20 or 50 unscored ‘beta’ questions).
  • Paper exam: usually 100 questions in 100 minutes.
  • Roughly one minute per question; you may review and change answers.
  • Candidates may attempt the exam up to three times in a 12-month period.

Preparing

The single most important resource is the SE Handbook itself — most people who pass have read it more than once. Academic Equivalency offers an alternative route to meet the knowledge requirement (for ASEP/CSEP) without sitting the exam, and INCOSE offers a short paid practice exam to preview the format.

Always verify
Exam content, fees, and requirements change over time. Confirm the current details on incose.org before relying on them.
